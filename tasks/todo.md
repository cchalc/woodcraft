# Woodcraft: AI-Powered Local Service Arbitrage

**Origin**: Greg Wood Concept Pitch (April 2026)
**Market**: White Rock / South Surrey, British Columbia
**Status**: Exploration & Ideation Phase

---

## The Core Idea

Use AI to intercept local service demand and capture the spread between what consumers pay and what providers charge. Build professional landing pages, run ads, capture leads, dispatch to vetted local providers, take 20-30% margin.

> "Most local service providers are exceptional at their trade but terrible at marketing."

---

## Why White Rock / South Surrey?

### Market Advantages
- **Affluent homeowners** — median home values $1.2M+, price-insensitive for quality work
- **Aging demographics** — older residents own homes but can't DIY; need reliable help
- **Underserved by big platforms** — Angi/HomeAdvisor focus on Vancouver proper
- **Community-oriented** — word of mouth matters; trust-based referrals
- **Clear geographic boundaries** — easy SEO targeting ("dryer vent cleaning White Rock")
- **Weather factors** — Pacific Northwest damp climate drives HVAC/maintenance demand

### Population
- White Rock: ~21,000
- South Surrey: ~80,000+
- Combined catchment: ~100,000 residents
- High percentage of single-family homeowners

### Local Competitors
- Most home service providers are small operations with poor online presence
- No dominant local lead-gen player
- Opportunity to become the "trusted local" brand

---

## Recommended Pilot Vertical: Dryer Vent Cleaning

### Why This First?
- **Low competition** — nobody is aggressively marketing this in the area
- **Fire safety anxiety** — resonates strongly with homeowners, especially older ones
- **Predictable demand** — annual maintenance cycle, not seasonal spikes
- **Simple fulfillment** — single service type, easy to quality-control
- **Good margins** — $150-300/job, providers happy at 70% (~$105-210)
- **Builds trust** — entry point before upselling HVAC, plumbing, electrical

### Unit Economics (Estimated)
| Component | Amount |
|-----------|--------|
| Customer pays | $200 |
| Provider receives (70%) | $140 |
| Gross margin | $60 |
| Ad cost (~15%) | -$30 |
| Platform/AI costs | -$5 |
| Net profit per job | **$25 (12.5%)** |

At 30 jobs/month = **$750/month net profit** from one vertical.

## Phase 1: Proof of Concept (Months 1-3)

### Week 1-2: Market Research & Provider Recruitment
- [ ] Identify 5-10 dryer vent cleaning providers in White Rock/South Surrey
- [ ] Cold call/visit local providers, pitch the value prop
- [ ] Negotiate provider agreements (80/20 split initially for loyalty)
- [ ] Verify licensing and insurance requirements (BC standards)

### Week 2-3: Build Landing Page
- [ ] Register domain (e.g., "semiahmooventcleaning.ca" or similar local name)
- [ ] Build Phoenix/LiveView landing page with:
  - [ ] Local trust signals (White Rock, South Surrey mentioned)
  - [ ] Fire safety messaging
  - [ ] Simple booking form (name, address, phone, preferred time)
  - [ ] AI chatbot for instant responses
- [ ] Set up Stripe Connect for payments

### Week 4-6: Launch & Test
- [ ] Start Google Local Service Ads (target: "dryer vent cleaning White Rock")
- [ ] SEO-optimize for long-tail queries
- [ ] Process first 10-20 jobs manually (learn the dispatch process)
- [ ] Collect customer reviews

### Week 8-12: Automate & Evaluate
- [ ] Implement AI chatbot for intake
- [ ] Automate provider dispatch and scheduling
- [ ] Analyze unit economics — is 15%+ net margin achievable?
- [ ] Go/no-go decision for second vertical

## Key Questions to Resolve

1. **Brand identity** — Should this feel like one local company or a network of specialists?
2. **Provider exclusivity** — Can we lock in exclusive relationships, or do providers work with multiple lead sources?
3. **Liability** — What insurance do we need? What happens if a provider damages property?
4. **BC licensing** — Do we need a business license to operate as a referral service? Any specific regulations?
5. **Payment flow** — Collect full payment upfront and pay providers, or let providers collect and invoice us?

## Tech Stack (Phoenix/Elixir)

### Why Phoenix?
- Real-time updates for job status (LiveView)
- Handles concurrent requests efficiently
- Easy to build AI integrations
- One developer can build and maintain the whole platform

### Core Features Needed
- Landing page per vertical
- Booking form with calendar integration
- AI chatbot (Claude API)
- Provider dashboard (job assignments, availability)
- Customer dashboard (booking history, receipts)
- Stripe Connect for split payments
- SMS/email notifications
- Review collection and sentiment analysis
